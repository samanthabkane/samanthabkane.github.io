Samantha B. Kane, PhD

Postdoctoral Research Associate, Annenberg Institute, Brown University

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at Brown University. My research is in the economics of education and labor economics. My work asks how public policies (e.g., education, health, environmental) affect outcomes in K-12, higher education, and the workforce using methods from applied microeconomics.

At Annenberg, I am developing research at the intersection of education and climate change. Before this position, I completed my PhD at Harvard University, where my dissertation examined the effect of reproductive rights laws on women's college choices. I also received my MPP from the Harvard Kennedy School and my B.A. from Princeton University's School of Public and International Affairs.

Previously, I worked in education policy at the U.S. Department of Education and a nonpartisan policy institute.
